--- title: "Consensus-Based Framework of Core Clinical Activities for Clinical Pharmacists in Mental Health" --- # Consensus-Based Framework of Core Clinical Activities ### *Final Criteria Set (n = 40)* **ESCP Mental Health Special Interest Group (SIG)** --- ## 🧩 Study Overview This framework represents the final consensus-based set of 40 clinical activities identified through a two‑round modified electronic Delphi (e‑Delphi) study involving **39 expert clinical pharmacists from 13 European countries**. The goal was to define the **core clinical activities** performed by clinical pharmacists working in mental health across diverse healthcare systems. This webpage provides an accessible dissemination tool for expert panelists, stakeholders, and members of the ESCP Mental Health SIG. --- ## 🔬 Methodology Summary - **Study design:** Modified electronic Delphi (e‑Delphi) - **Rounds:** Two - **Participants:** 39 clinical pharmacists (13 countries) - **Eligibility:** ≥3 years of clinical pharmacy practice in mental health - **Rating scale:** 4‑point Likert - **Consensus definition:** - ≥80% agreement - Median ≥3 - IQR ≤1 A total of **40 out of 45 criteria reached full consensus** and form the final framework. --- # ✅ Final Consensus Criteria Below are the **40 final consensus-based clinical activities**, organised into seven domains. --- # **Domain 1 — Medicines Optimisation**
Expand Domain 1 1. Conduct comprehensive medication reviews for individuals with mental illness, including appropriateness of indication, drug selection, duration, interactions, and prevention of adverse effects. 2. Prioritise structured medication review in older adults or individuals with multimorbidities, polypharmacy, frailty, or high‑risk psychotropic use. 3. Use validated tools (e.g., Beers, STOPP/START) to recommend safer alternatives and minimise prescribing cascades. 4. Provide medication reviews across primary and secondary care settings. 5. Ensure medicines reconciliation across transitions, including complete history and discrepancy resolution. 6. Provide seamless care during admission, discharge, and other transitions. 7. Support adherence through tailored interventions and shared decision‑making (e.g., LAI support, adverse effect management). 8. Manage clinically significant interactions (drug–drug, drug–gene, drug–disease). 9. Optimise use of high‑risk psychotropics (clozapine, LAIs, high‑dose antipsychotics) with monitoring compliance. 10. Use pharmacokinetic and pharmacogenomic tools to optimise prescribing. 11. Make therapeutic substitutions when appropriate while ensuring equivalent efficacy and safety. 12. Provide interventions at multiple points of care, including during transitions.
--- # **Domain 2 — Physical Health Monitoring and Interventions**
Expand Domain 2 13. Recognise and flag physical–mental health interdependencies relevant to pharmacotherapy. 14. Recommend and implement physical health and lifestyle interventions (e.g., smoking cessation, weight management, screening). 15. Use validated tools to identify physical health risks and guide recommendations. 16. Support monitoring and optimisation of chronic conditions in individuals receiving psychotropics.
--- # **Domain 3 — Person-Centred Care and Counselling**
Expand Domain 3 17. Provide person‑centred pharmaceutical care, involving individuals, families, or carers in shared decision‑making. 18. Provide medication counselling and medicines-related education, respecting cognitive and health literacy needs.
--- # **Domain 4 — Interprofessional Collaboration and Care Coordination**
Expand Domain 4 19. Integrate into multidisciplinary teams to provide specialised pharmaceutical input. 20. Collaborate across care settings to ensure continuity of care, especially during transitions. 21. Document and communicate care plans and outcomes to support collaborative decision‑making. 22. Engage in professional networks (local, national, international) to support service development and advocacy. 23. Contribute to discussions on funding or reimbursement models relevant to mental health pharmacy services.
--- # **Domain 5 — Advanced and Extended Roles**
Expand Domain 5 24. Facilitate medicines‑focused group interventions (education, adherence support, adverse effect management). 25. Provide community post‑discharge services, including medicines reconciliation and outpatient medication reviews. 26. Optimise the use of new technologies supporting medication-related decision‑making (CDSS, e‑prescribing, pharmacogenomics).
--- # **Domain 6 — Administrative and Governance Activities**
Expand Domain 6 27. Manage references and monitor high‑risk medications to ensure safe use. 28. Conduct audits of prescribing practices and adherence to best practice standards. 29. Contribute to formulary decision‑making. 30. Develop and review guidelines and decision-support tools to promote safe prescribing. 31. Implement relevant local and national practice recommendations within clinical teams.
--- # **Domain 7 — Research, Education, Service Development & Quality Improvement**
Expand Domain 7 32. Lead or participate in research, including clinical trials. 33. Collaborate with academic and professional networks on research in mental health pharmacy. 34. Provide education and mentorship to support evidence‑based practice. 35. Undertake quality improvement and service development projects. 36. Establish and monitor quality indicators for mental health pharmacy services. 37. Teach clinical pharmacy in mental health within undergraduate, postgraduate, or interprofessional programmes. 38. Supervise undergraduate research, including theses or dissertations. 39. Conduct projects supporting innovation, service development, and reimbursement models. 40. Disseminate service evaluation findings to healthcare leaders, policymakers, and the public.
