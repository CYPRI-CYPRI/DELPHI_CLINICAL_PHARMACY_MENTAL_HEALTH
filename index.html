Key Activities and Roles of Clinical Pharmacists Working in Mental Health

This page presents the final consensus-based framework developed through a European modified e-Delphi study conducted within the Mental Health Special Interest Group of the European Society of Clinical Pharmacy (ESCP).

Study overview

The aim of the study was to establish a structured, consensus-based framework of key clinical activities for clinical pharmacists working in mental health across Europe. The study involved experts from 13 European countries.

Background

Clinical pharmacy services in mental health have developed heterogeneously across Europe. Although competency frameworks exist, a clearly structured and agreed European list of core clinical activities in routine mental health pharmacy practice has been lacking.

What this page shows

Below is the final set of consensus-based criteria organised into seven domains. These domains reflect the main areas in which clinical pharmacists contribute to safe, effective, and person-centred psychopharmacotherapy.

Final consensus domains

The final framework comprises seven domains covering medicines optimisation, physical health monitoring, person-centred care, interprofessional collaboration, advanced roles, governance, and research or service development.

D1
Medicines Optimisation
⌄
  1. Conduct comprehensive medication reviews for individuals with mental illness, including pharmacotherapy across specialties.
  2. Prioritise structured medication review in older adults and other high-risk populations with multimorbidity, frailty, or polypharmacy.
  3. Use validated tools such as Beers and STOPP/START within comprehensive medication reviews.
  4. Provide medication reviews in both primary and secondary care settings.
  5. Ensure medication reconciliation and resolution of discrepancies across transitions of care.
  6. Provide seamless care during admission, transfer, and discharge.
  7. Support adherence and shared decision-making through tailored interventions.
  8. Manage clinically significant drug-drug, drug-gene, and drug-disease interactions.
  9. Optimise use of high-risk psychotropics including clozapine and high-dose antipsychotics.
  10. Optimise prescribing using pharmacokinetic and pharmacogenomic tools where appropriate.
  11. Make therapeutic substitutions where appropriate while preserving efficacy and safety.
  12. Provide interventions at multiple points of care, including transitions and discharge.
D2
Physical Health Monitoring and Interventions
⌄
  1. Recognise and flag physical–mental health interdependencies relevant to pharmacotherapy.
  2. Recommend and implement physical health and lifestyle interventions, including preventive screening and risk-reduction support.
  3. Use relevant tools to identify physical health risks and modifiable lifestyle factors.
  4. Support monitoring and optimisation of chronic conditions in people receiving psychotropic medication.
D3
Person-Centred Care and Counselling
⌄
  1. Provide person-centred pharmaceutical care involving individuals and, where appropriate, families or carers in shared decision-making.
  2. Provide medication counselling and medicines-related education that respects cognitive and health literacy needs.
D4
Interprofessional Collaboration and Care Coordination
⌄
  1. Integrate into multidisciplinary teams to provide expert pharmaceutical input.
  2. Collaborate across care settings with pharmacists and other healthcare professionals to ensure continuity and safety of treatment.
  3. Document and communicate care plans, actions, and outcomes in a timely manner.
  4. Engage in professional networks and representative groups at local, national, and international level.
  5. Contribute, where feasible, to discussions on funding or reimbursement models for mental health pharmacy services.
D5
Advanced and Extended Roles
⌄
  1. Facilitate medicines-focused group-based interventions where appropriate within scope of practice.
  2. Offer community post-discharge services, including medicines reconciliation and outpatient medication reviews.
  3. Optimise the use of new technologies to support medication-related decision-making.
D6
Administrative and Governance Activities
⌄
  1. Manage references and monitor high-risk medications to ensure safe use.
  2. Conduct audits of prescribing practices and adherence to best practice standards.
  3. Contribute to formulary decision-making regarding approved medications.
  4. Develop and review clinical practice guidelines, pharmacy guidance, and decision-support tools.
  5. Implement local and national practice recommendations across clinical teams and services.
D7
Research, Education, Service Development and Quality Improvement
⌄
  1. Lead or participate in research, including clinical trials relevant to mental health pharmacy.
  2. Collaborate within academic and professional networks to conduct research in mental health pharmacy.
  3. Provide education and mentorship to advance evidence-based practice.
  4. Undertake quality improvement and service development projects.
  5. Establish and monitor quality indicators demonstrating service effectiveness.
  6. Teach clinical pharmacy in mental health within undergraduate and postgraduate programmes.
  7. Supervise undergraduate research including thesis or dissertation supervision.
  8. Conduct projects supporting innovation, service development, and reimbursement models.
  9. Disseminate service evaluation results to stakeholders, including healthcare leaders and policymakers.
